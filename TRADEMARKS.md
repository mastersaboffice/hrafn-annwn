## Permitted Use: Nominative Fair Use

You may refer to these names and marks to describe accurate, factual 
relationships, provided you do not imply sponsorship, endorsement, or 
official affiliation.

**Permitted examples:**
- "This implementation is based on Daemon Architecture (DA)"
- "Compatible with Gothic Tech Druidism framework"
- "Implements consciousness architecture from Hrafn Annwn"
- "Uses principles from the Ravens of the Otherworld project"
- "Tested against Daemon Architecture specifications"

**Not permitted:**
- Using marks as your product/service name
- Implying official endorsement
- Creating confusion about source
- Suggesting you are the original project

## Renaming Requirement for Derivatives

**If you distribute forks, derivatives, or hosted services based on this 
work, you MUST use distinct product names and branding.**

**You may NOT use the protected marks in:**
- Product titles or names
- Service names
- Domain names
- Company names
- App store listings
- Marketing materials

**You MUST:**
- Choose a distinct name for your derivative
- Clearly indicate it is "based on" or "derived from" the original
- Include proper attribution per LICENSE requirements
- Avoid creating confusion with the original project

**Compliant examples:**
- "Consciousness Stack" (based on Daemon Architecture from Hrafn Annwn)
- "TechDruid Framework" (implementing Gothic Tech Druidism principles)
- "Neural Covenant" (derived from Ravens of the Otherworld architecture)

**Non-compliant examples:**
- "Hrafn Annwn Community Edition" (uses protected mark)
- "Gothic Tech Druidism Plus" (uses protected mark)
- "DaemonChat Pro" (uses protected mark)
- "Ravens Cloud" (uses protected mark)
